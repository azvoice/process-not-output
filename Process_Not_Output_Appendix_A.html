The 193-Law Tagged Dataset

Process / Output / Conduct Taxonomy of Enacted State AI Statutes

This Appendix enumerates all 193 enacted state AI statutes coded in the dataset underlying Part III of the accompanying Comment. Each row lists the state, the statute’s name or bill number (hyperlinked to the primary source where available), the citation, the effective date, the subject-matter category from the underlying compilation, and the regulatory-modality bucket assigned by the author.

The Process / Output / Conduct / Mixed taxonomy is the author’s original analytical contribution. The underlying compilation of enacted statutes is drawn from Ted Claypoole et al., Think Like a Lawyer About AI, ABA Bus. L. Sec. Cyber & Tech. L. Comm. (Feb. 17, 2026).

NYC Local Law 144, a municipal ordinance, is excluded from this count and appears in the Comment as a comparator.

Methodology Note on Citation Field

Citations marked with an asterisk (*) are inherited from the underlying CLE compilation and contain inconsistencies that pre-date this Comment. Specifically, 6 citations in the source compilation list state-code references that do not match the state of the enacting jurisdiction (e.g., a Wyoming code citation appearing in a Vermont row). The citations are reproduced as-received with the asterisk indicator; readers requiring authoritative citations for those statutes should consult the linked primary source for each row.

The de-duplication of New York’s RAISE Act and the correction of its bill numbering and effective date follow the verified Bluebook citation: N.Y. Gen. Bus. Law §§ 1420–1425, enacted by S.B. 6953B / A.B. 6453B, signed Dec. 19, 2025, effective Jan. 1, 2027.

Bucket assignments and totals (Conduct 84 / Output 60 / Process 31 / Mixed 18 = 193) are unaffected by these notes.
