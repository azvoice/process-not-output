Section 8(b) Crosswalk

Executive Order 14365 § 8(b) Reservations Mapped to State AI Law Buckets

Section 8(b) of Executive Order 14365 reserves four categories of state law from federal preemption: child safety, AI computing infrastructure, state government procurement, and other categories to be determined. This figure maps each reservation to the regulatory-modality buckets developed in § III.C of the accompanying Comment.

The principal finding is that § 8(b)’s reservations overlap heavily with the CONDUCT bucket. The Order’s own carve-outs are already, in substance, conduct-bucket protections.

Fig. 3 — EO § 8(b) reservations mapped to bucket categories. Ribbon thickness indicates approximate overlap weight.

Sparse direct match. No enacted state statute in the 193-law dataset directly regulates AI compute as a discrete subject. Two MIXED statutes (AZ Computational Power Law, MT Right to Compute Act) regulate local-government authority over compute, peripherally.
EO § 8(b)(iii)
State Government Procurement
State-agency AI use rules; government-procurement transparency (CA, MD, NH, TX, VA)
CONDUCT · 100%
Bucket overlap
Pure CONDUCT overlap. State procurement rules govern how state agencies buy and use AI for their own operations — the state acting as market participant, not as regulator of private speech.
